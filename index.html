Where Legacies are Built

Every Muslim should ponder these three essential questions: what will be your lasting memory? How will you make a difference to the Ummah? What legacy will you leave behind?

Say hello to Akhr, a dynamic platform designed for those who aspire to create a lasting impact and invest in their hereafter through acts of kindness and charitable endeavors. With Akhr, you’ll be empowered to forge a meaningful legacy that resonates throughout time and benefits the Ummah for generations to come.

Akhr in the Arabic language has multiple meanings. Short for al-Ākhirah (Arabic: الآخرة, derived from Akhir which means last, ultimate, end or close) is an Arabic term for “the Hereafter”.

Introducing Phase 1

Brotherhood & Impactful Connections

As we embark on the AKHR journey, we’re kicking off with a powerful theme that resonates with every member: Brotherhood.

Inspired by the words of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him), “None of you will believe until you love for your brother what you love for yourself,”

We’re setting out to create a thriving online community of dedicated brothers and sisters. By making a small and regular contribution, we’ll nurture a support system that brings believers together in a show of solidarity and brotherhood.

Phase 1 features

Burial service

Wherever possible we aim to cover the burial costs of our members. This service is reserved specifically for our most needy members especially revert Muslims who may have no support or Muslim family.

Hajj and Umrah package

Upon confirmation of your departure to the hereafter we will immediately initiate a umrah or hajj trip on your behalf, depending on your membership status and availability.

Sadaqah Jariyah

Imagine been part of a Sadaqah Jariyah that’s always growing. The idea is to keep the movement going from generation to generation until Yawm Al Qiyamah. From your monthly subscription we will pay Sadaqah Jariyah on behalf of all members.

Waqf endowment

A waqf fund is a charitable endowment in Islamic tradition where the donated funds are used for social welfare causes. Donating to a waqf fund is considered a virtuous act in Islam as it serves as a perpetual form of charity, providing ongoing benefits to the community and earning reward for the donor in the afterlife.
